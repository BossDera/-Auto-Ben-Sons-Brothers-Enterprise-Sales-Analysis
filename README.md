# Auto-Ben Sons & Brothers Enterprise Sales Analysis
## Description
> This Excel project analyzes the sales performance of Auto-Ben Sons & Brothers Enterprise using transactional sales data. The objective of this analysis is to generate meaningful insights that support business decision-making, revenue tracking, and product performance evaluation.
.
	
## Project Overview
> This project evaluates the company’s end-of-year sales performance across multiple products and categories.
The dashboard presents key metrics such as total revenue, average revenue, maximum and minimum sales values, and total units sold. Visualizations including bar charts, pie charts, and pivot tables help stakeholders easily understand sales trends and product contributions

## Data Content
### Rows
> Sales records representing product transactions.
### Columns

Key dataset fields include:

•	Product Name

•	Product Category

•	Units Sold

•	Revenue

•	Product Type

•	Sales Region

•	Sales Performance Metrics


## Tool Used
### Microsoft Excel

Features used:

•	Pivot Tables

•	Pivot Charts

•	Excel Formulas

•	Dashboard Design

•	Data Aggregation & Summarization

## Steps
1. Total Revenue Calculation
Pivot Table:
Values → Revenue → Sum
Result displayed in dashboard KPI card.

2. Average Revenue
Pivot Table:
Values → Revenue → Average
Provides insight into the average sales value per transaction.

3. Maximum Revenue
Pivot Table:
Values → Revenue → Max
Used to identify the highest sales record.

4. Minimum Revenue
Pivot Table:
Values → Revenue → Min
Shows the lowest recorded sales amount.

5. Total Units Sold
Pivot Table:
Values → Units Sold → Sum
Displays the total number of products sold.

6. Sales by Product
Rows: Product Name
Values: Units Sold → Sum
Visualized using a bar chart to show product demand.

7. Sales by Category
Rows: Category
Values: Units Sold → Sum
Helps determine which category sells the most.

8. Revenue Distribution by Product
Rows: Product Name
Values: Revenue → Sum
Displayed in a pie chart to highlight product contribution.

9. Revenue by Category
Rows: Category
Values: Revenue → Sum
Displayed using a column chart to compare category performance.

10. Product Performance Comparison
Rows: Product Name
Values:
•	Units Sold
•	Revenue
This helps identify top-performing products.

## Findings
1.	The total revenue generated reached 302.6 million, showing strong annual sales performance. 💰
2.	Certain products contributed significantly more to total revenue compared to others.
3.	Some categories showed higher sales volumes, indicating stronger customer demand.
4.	A few products recorded very low sales, suggesting opportunities for marketing improvement or product revaluation.
5.	The revenue distribution chart shows uneven contribution across products, with a few top performers driving most sales.
